UNC Computer Science Undergraduate Learning Assistant - 2020 to 2021

    For Fall 2020 and Spring 2021, I was an undergraduate learning assistant (ULA) for the UNC Computer Science Department. The class that I was a ULA for was COMP 210: Data Structures, one of the introductory classes in the computer science curriculum. Data structures is a class that is incredibly important because a solid understanding will help greatly in problem solving, classes, and projects.

Responsibilities

As a ULA, my primary responsibility was to hold office hours to assist students master the course material as well as aid in any technical issues students may have. Over the course of two semesters, I held over 160+ office hours sessions with more than 200+ student interactions. Sometimes students had simple issues that would be fixed very easily but othertimes they would be highly complex issues that could take a long time. From these experiences I improved on my methods for explaining concepts to students and became more efficient at debugging so that I could more quickly help students.
