SAS Institute - Summer 2021 to Present

    During the summer of 2021 I was a technical intern on the Business Graphics team in the Visualization Department at SAS Institute. I worked on a project to create an interactive version of a continuous legend meant to be used in SAS software products like Visual Analytics.

  • At the beginning, I was handed an extensive design specification and worked with my manager to break it down into achievable goals that I could work towards. Throughout my 12 weeks during the summer, I was able to implement a variety of features. At the end of the summer, I presented my work to the Data Visualization R&D Group at SAS including the Director of Data Visualization R&D and the Vice President of Visualiztion. I also presented my work at the SAS Intern Expo to other interns and employees. In total, I was able to showcase my work to 100+ people, many of whom are frequent users of or developers for SAS Visual Analytics.

Interactive Continuous Legend Features

Features include:

  • Range Attributes Maps - a collection of entries with properties such as min and max values, start and end colors, and more.
  • Extends the existing continuous legend that allows it to be integrated seamlessly into existing applications
  • Draggable sliders to change color ramp
  • Optional custom range attributes map a user can tailor to their data set
  • Optional histogram to show the data distibution
  • Option to extend the min and max colors instead of overflow and underflow colors

During my time I SAS I learned a lot about what software development at a large corporation is like. I attended weekly scrum meetings, learned internal processes and tools, and fell into the Agile development cycle. Additionally, I learned many technical skills including learning C++ from scratch, debugging when there are thousands of lines of code, and how to thoroughly test my code before pushing it out. ALthough my summer internship ended, I am contining to work for SAS as a year-round technical intern where I can both continue my studies and gain experience as a software developer.
