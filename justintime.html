Just In Time App for UNC Center for Nursing Excellence

    During my Falls 2022 COMP 523 class, Software Engineering Lab, we were tasked to create a cross-platform mobile app for our client, the UNC Center for Nursing Excellence. Due to everchanging policies, procedures, and resources for patient treatment they wanted something that would give nurses timely updates.

  • My team and I met with our client to see what kind of project they had in mind and it turned out to be a mobile app. None my team members and I had much previous experience with mobile apps and our client didn't know much about technology either so we consulted the UNC App Lab to see what kind of technology might best fit out project. We had done some research and thought a React Native app and a Firebase backend would be suitable and the App Lab agreed.

    After coming up with a UI design and having it approved by our client we began working on the technical aspects. The project was split into two parts. The first was the React Native mobile app which would be user facing and the second would be the React website that administrators would use. These two sections of the project were split between our team and I ended up working on the React Native mobile app.

    I worked to help construct the UI to make sure it looked like our UI mockups. In addition I worked on connecting the Firebase backend to the mobile app. A team member who had been working on the administrator website had already connected that website to the backend but I had to make modifications to ensure that it would work with the React Native mobile app.

    Over the course of the semester my team and I went from UI mockups to a fully functioning mobile application. We presented our work to our client who were thrilled about the progress that we had made.

Just In Time App

Features include:

  • React administrator website
